# Review Writing Style

## Hard Constraints

- Do not use em dashes.
- Use the paper's own notation. Do not invent symbols or rename variables unless the paper already
  does so.
- Do not force every point into the same bold-label-plus-colon template.
- Do not overuse stock AI phrases such as "it remains unclear", "is a sensible design", "not
  convincingly supported", or "consistent with the motivation".

## Preferred Voice

Write like a human reviewer: direct, concise, and willing to make a plain judgment when the record
supports it.

Examples:

- Prefer "the paper does not explain this choice" over "it remains unclear why this was done."
- Prefer "I find this unconvincing" over polished but vague hedging.
- Prefer "works well in practice" over "is a sensible design."
- Prefer "the experiments fall short of validating this claim" over "is not convincingly
  supported."

## Rhythm And Sentence Variety

- Avoid making every paragraph start with a neat summary sentence followed by symmetrical expansion.
- Some paragraphs should open from concrete evidence: a table, an ablation result, an equation, or
  a mismatch between claim and setup.
- Vary question phrasing. Do not repeat the same "How...?" pattern for every question.

## Consistency Checks Before Finalizing

- Re-read the strengths after writing the weaknesses. Remove praise that directly conflicts with
  your own criticisms.
- If you say the ablations are transparent, that does not mean they support the paper's claim.
- Keep the wording reviewer-facing, not assistant-facing.
